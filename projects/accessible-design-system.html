Problem

Problem

Teams were shipping interfaces with inconsistent patterns and varying levels of accessibility support, making it hard to guarantee inclusive experiences.

Research & Insights

Research & Insights

We audited existing products and studied WCAG guidelines to identify where components and content patterns were falling short.

Component audit
Accessibility checklist

Strategy

Strategy

We focused on a core set of high‑impact components and defined clear usage guidance so teams could adopt accessible defaults quickly.

Design Exploration

Design Exploration

States, focus treatments, and color tokens were explored in parallel to ensure contrast, clarity, and flexibility across different brands.

Component states
Token scales

Testing

Testing

We ran quick checks with screen readers and keyboard navigation to validate that new patterns held up beyond static mocks.

Final Solution

Final Solution

The resulting system shipped with accessible components, usage examples, and hand‑off documentation that product teams could plug into quickly.

Results

Results

Designers reported faster hand‑offs and fewer late‑stage accessibility issues once the system was adopted.

Reflection

Reflection

This project reinforced how much clarity a well‑documented, accessible system can bring to product work across teams.
