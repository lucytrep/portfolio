Overview

Rethinking How Marketplace Conversations Work

Facebook Marketplace connects billions of users, but its chat experience hasn’t evolved to support the scale of interactions happening within it. Buyers and sellers are left navigating cluttered inboxes, unclear message order, and fragmented workflows.

This project rethinks Marketplace chat as a structured transaction space—introducing AI summaries, queue visibility, and integrated actions to help users move from conversation to completion more efficiently.

The Problem

Conversations Without Structure Create Friction

Marketplace messaging is chaotic. Sellers receive dozens of messages with no clear order, while buyers compete for attention without knowing where they stand.

Important signals—like intent, urgency, and availability—are buried inside threads. As a result, users move conversations off-platform, creating fragmented and inefficient transactions.

Research Insights

What Breaks Down in Real Transactions

I conducted user interviews and system analysis to understand how people buy and sell through Marketplace, and where communication breaks down.

Messages lack structure Users can’t easily track who messaged first, who is still active, or which threads are closest to completing a transaction.
Too much back-and-forth Simple transactions require excessive messaging to clarify basics like availability, price, or pickup timing.
Trust is unclear Buyers and sellers struggle to identify serious participants, leading to stalled conversations and abandoned threads.

Defining the Opportunity

Turning Messaging Into a Decision-Making Tool

How might we transform Marketplace chat into a system that not only hosts conversations, but actively supports decision-making and transaction flow?

Make transaction state visible Elevate information like “who messaged first,” “who’s waiting,” and “what’s next” so users can quickly prioritize.
Reduce manual coordination Bring key actions—like scheduling, confirming price, and sharing pickup details—directly into the conversation space.
Use AI where it truly helps Leverage AI to summarize intent and highlight important signals, without replacing human judgment or creating false certainty.

Design Direction

Structuring Chat Around Action, Not Just Messages

I reframed Marketplace chat as more than messaging—it becomes a transaction layer that guides buyers and sellers from inquiry to completion.

Conversation as a queue Threads are organized around transaction status and recency, helping sellers see who is next in line and who has dropped off.
AI-supported, not AI-led AI surfaces summaries, key asks, and unresolved details so users can act faster without digging through long histories.
Actions inside the thread Scheduling, confirming price, and sharing logistics live inside the conversation, so users don’t have to jump across surfaces to move forward.

Final Product Experience

A System That Moves Users From Message to Match

The final experience treats Marketplace chat as a structured transaction space, supported by AI summaries, queue visibility, and integrated actions.

Buyers and sellers can quickly understand who they’re talking to, what’s been agreed on, and what needs to happen next—whether that’s confirming price, scheduling pickup, or completing payment.

Impact

Faster Decisions, Fewer Drop-Offs

The redesigned system reduces message overload by prioritizing active, high-intent conversations and making transaction state easier to understand at a glance.

By structuring chat around clear actions and AI-supported summaries, buyers and sellers can make decisions faster, with fewer abandoned threads and off-platform detours.

Reflection

What Designing at Marketplace Scale Taught Me

Designing messaging at scale is about coordination, not just communication.

Design the System Creating value in Marketplace chat meant defining how conversations move, not only how messages look.
Humans + AI Roles AI is most useful when it summarizes and highlights, while people stay in control of decisions and tone.
Flexibility + Focus Giving teams room to experiment works best when it happens inside clear constraints that protect the user experience.
